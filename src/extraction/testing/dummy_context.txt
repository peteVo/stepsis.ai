--- SECTION: Results ---
A total of 450 patients with suspected sepsis were included in the final analysis. The overall 28-day mortality rate was 22.4% (101 patients). Patients in the non-survivor group had significantly higher initial lactate levels compared to survivors (median 4.2 mmol/L [IQR 2.8-6.1] vs 1.9 mmol/L [IQR 1.2-2.5], p < 0.001). Multivariable logistic regression showed that initial lactate > 4.0 mmol/L was independently associated with 28-day mortality (OR 3.42, 95% CI 2.15-5.30).
